# Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal personal information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical case, a student receives an email that looks like it is from a bank or a popular website. The email creates urgency and asks the user to click on a link and enter their details. When the user clicks the link, it opens a fake website that looks real. If the user enters their information, it is directly sent to the attacker.

Students and online users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms and may not verify every message carefully. The consequences can include loss of money, hacked accounts, and misuse of personal data.

This case shows how important it is to think before clicking on links and always verify the authenticity of messages.
